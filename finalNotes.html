Chapter Six

Moral Philosophy

Refers to the specific principles or values people use to decide what is right and wrong

Economic value orientationy

if an act produces more value for its effort, then it should be accepted as ethical.

Idealism

is a moral philosophy that places special value on ideas and ideals as products of the mind

Realism

is the view that an external world exists independent of our perceptions.

Monists

believe only one thing is intrinsically good.

Pluralists

believe two or more things are intriniscally good.

Hedonism

the ideal that pleasure is the ultimate good.

Teleology

stipulates acts are morally right or acceptable if they produce some desired result, such as realization of self-interest or utility

Egoism

Defines right or acceptable actions as those that maximise a particular person's self-interest as defined by the individual.

Utiliarianism

Defines right or acceptable actions as those that maximize total utility, or the greatest good for the greatest number of people.

Deotology

Focuses on the preservation of individuals rights and on the intentions associated with particular behavior rather than on its consequences.

Relavist

Focuses on the preservation of individuals rights and on the intentions associated with particular behavior rather than on its consequences.

Virture ethics

Assumes what is moral in a given situation is not only what conventional morality requires but also what the mature person with a "good" moral character deems appropriate.

Justice

Evaluates ethicalness on the basis of fairness: distributive, procedural, and interactional.

Instrumentalists

They reject the ideas that:

  1. Ends can be seperated from the means that produce them
  2. Ends, purposes, or outcomes are intrinsically good in and of themselves.

Chapter Seven

Apathetic Culture

Exhibits high concern for people minimal concern for performance issues.

Caring Culture

Shows little concern for people but high concern for performance with a focus on the interest of an organization.

Exacting Culture

shows little concern for people but a high concern for performance with a focus on the interest of an organization.

Integrative Culture

Combines a high concern for people and performance

Cultural Audit

Is an assessment of an organization's values

Compliance Culture

Is organized around risk, using a legalistic approach to ethics.

Value-Based Ethics Culture

relies upon an explicity mission statement that deefines the core values of the firm and how customers employees should be treated.
